Lightning causes a 35-minute delay on North-South Line

30 Sep 2013



An electrical failure on the North-South Line was caused by a lightning during Sunday morning's thunderstorm, which led to a 35-minute delay.

Train service on the North-South Line was disrupted yesterday morning after a southbound train encountered an electrical failure.

Train operator SMRT said in a Facebook statement that the fault was caused by lightning that had struck somewhere in the area around Kranji station during the morning thunderstorm.

The affected train stalled at Woodlands station at around 6:40am, and was pushed back to the depot by another empty train. SMRT added that the incident caused a 35-minute delay and affected some 3,500 passengers.
